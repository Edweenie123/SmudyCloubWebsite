Intro

Making an effective wind turbine

Most wind turbines are based upon a common principle - Energy in the form of wind is used to turn a number of blades which work together to spin a generator. When the generator is spun, it transforms kinetic energy into electrical energy. The propeller-like blades of an effective wind turbine must have a very specific shape; this shape is called a curved blade. When air flows through this shape, it creates an area of low pressure and high pressure causing the blade to spin. An efficient wind turbine should also have a gear box that is spun by the rotational energy of the blades. The gear box is used to multiply the RPM of wind turbine, thus creating more electricity.

Design

Process:

We began with the idea that to have an efficient wind turbine, we would need to have a gearbox.  We began by taking different size gears and tinkering until we realized that the combination of large and small gears was best.  We then set out to make the bow itself. The first problem was figuring out how to configure the gears, however we figured out the optimal positions pretty quickly. Then next problem was trying to actually get the sticks in the correct spot so the gear would link together with it. We tried eyeingballing it but that failed so we resorted to using a ruler and measuring the distance between the centre of the gears. Once we had figured where the sticks would go, the rest of the building process was very easy.  The wings would follow and then the base was connected to the box and base. Some things that we had considered but ended not using was the use of paper wings. Initially, we used paper wings however we realised that it underperformed as it flopped around too much. This realisation helped us begin to use cardboard instead and we eventually created our current wings.

Conclusion

How did we do?

Ultimately, our windmill was pretty successful. The base provided support and it was stable enough to withstand the wind. The winds spun fast and the gears in the gearbox spun with little resistance. This allowed our windmill to produce a 1.28 voltage. To improve our windmill, we could put the gears on the outside of the gearbox. The gears could act as stoppers, leaning against the box and provide stability. This would also let the gears be adjusted easier, allowing quick changes and modifications.  More gears could also be added if the gears were outside the box as well. This would increase the speed of the small gear attached to the motor and increase the electricity production. The resistance could also be reduced by lubing the connection points between the dowels and the boxes with clear tape. Although we lubed it, wood has a lot of resistance when it is spun on wood. Thus, by employing clear tape, the dowels would be spinning against the tape instead of the cardboard, allowing for very minimal resistance. The wings could also be more aerodynamic. This would be done by bending the cardboard or using other materials such as plastic or metal.

what are some Additional improvements?

After our initial testing, we decided to further improve our design. We tried carrying out one of the implementations of change stated in the conclusion, namely the change in the gearbox. Other changes such as minimizing the resistance through scotch tape would allow the gears to spin faster and more efficiently contributing to a higher electricity production. Making the wings more aerodynamic would also allow the wings to catch more air, spinning the dowel connected to the wings faster.
